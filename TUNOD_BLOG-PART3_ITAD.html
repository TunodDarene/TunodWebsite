Behind the scenes of Gaming

by: Darene Hanz H. Tunod

What caught my interest

I used to think games were just something to play when I wanted to relax or pass time. I never really thought about what happens behind the screen. As I continued studying IT, I realized that games are not just about fun. They are built using logic, programming, and a lot of problem-solving. Making even a simple game requires planning, testing, and patience. I realized how complicated making a video game can be. From coding mechanics, designing levels, and creating characters to making sure everything works together, the process is really detailed and complex.

        I started paying attention not only to playing games, but also to how they work and how they are created. The more I learned about game mechanics, the more curious I became about what happens behind the screen. I started to wonder how games were made on older hardware and with simpler tools. It amazed me to see how creative developers were, even with technical limitations. Thinking about the effort and teamwork that goes into producing a game gave me a new respect for game developers.

Exploring Options

At first, I just enjoyed playing games during free time. But as I learned more about programming and debugging, I began to notice how much work goes into making even a simple game. I realized that creating games requires both technical skills and creativity. Developers need to combine art, coding, sound, and design thinking to create something engaging and functional.

        Even though I enjoy games and learning about how they are made, I’ve realized that I don’t want to pursue game development as my main career. Instead, I want to focus on other areas of IT, like software development, web development, or cybersecurity. I see game development as a fun side gig, a place where I can experiment and apply the skills I’m already learning in school.

        Currently, I’m exploring programs like Roblox Studio and Blender. With Roblox Studio, I practice scripting, designing levels, and thinking about player interactions. Blender allows me to model characters and objects, and even create animations. These programs give me a hands-on way to learn and make mistakes safely. I can experiment, try different ideas, and see the results instantly. It’s exciting to see my creations come to life, even if they are small or simple.

Baby Steps

Even though I don’t plan to make game development my main career, exploring it as a fun side project has taught me a lot about patience, creativity, and problem-solving. Using Roblox Studio and Blender has shown me how much effort goes into building a game, and it has also helped me practice programming and technical skills in a more engaging way.

        I’ve realized that it’s okay not to have all the answers or a fully decided career path. What matters is trying, experimenting, and improving little by little. Each small project I complete teaches me something new, whether it’s about coding, design, or even just time management.

        For now, I plan to focus on my main IT career while continuing to explore game development in my free time. I see it as a creative playground where I can apply my skills, challenge myself, and even collaborate with friends or other hobbyists. Game development is a learning journey I can enjoy at my own pace, and it keeps me motivated to explore other areas of IT and technology as well.

Looking Ahead

As I continue my studies and grow my skills in IT, I hope to use what I learn not only in games but in other projects too. Experimenting with game development has opened my eyes to how software can be fun, interactive, and meaningful. Even small projects can teach lessons about problem-solving, creativity, and patience that I can carry into my main career.

        In the future, I hope to combine my IT skills with hobbies like game development to create projects that are both useful and enjoyable. Whether it’s designing a simple educational game, a web app, or exploring new programming languages, I want to keep experimenting and growing. The skills I develop through gaming projects are transferable to other areas of IT, and every small step I take now builds a stronger foundation for the future.
