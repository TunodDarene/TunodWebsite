My Recap

by: Darene Hanz H. Tunod

Childhood

      1My name is Darene Hanz H. Tunod. 2I was born in Quezon City, but I live in Caloocan. 3I grew up in a simple and loving family where we valued respect and responsibility. 4My childhood was quiet, peaceful, and filled with small but meaningful lessons. 5I was usually shy around new people, and I did not talk much unless I felt comfortable. 6Even though I was shy, I was observant and liked watching how people acted and treated each other. 7My parents played a big role in shaping who I am today. 8They taught me to be polite, responsible, and respectful at all times. 9I learned that even small actions, like saying “thank you” or helping at home, matter a lot. 10From Grade 1 to Grade 6, I studied at OB Montessori in Quezon City, Fairview. 11School helped me slowly build my confidence. 12I liked reading and writing because it allowed me to express myself without having to speak too much. 13I also enjoyed playing with friends during recess, even though I usually stayed close to a small group. 14One of my favorite hobbies when I was young was swimming. 15My parents enrolled me in swimming lessons to make sure I would learn an important life skill. 16At first, I was scared of the water. 17I did not like the feeling of floating and not being able to stand properly. 18But my instructor was patient and guided me step by step. 19He taught me how to float, breathe properly, and move my arms and legs correctly. 20Swimming lessons taught me how to face my fears and trust myself. 21On weekends, I often visited my grandparents. 22I spent time with my cousins, and we would play simple games together. 23Those moments were special because they helped me learn how to socialize and adjust to different personalities. 24I also enjoyed quiet time at home, watching TV or just resting. 25Looking back, my childhood may have been simple, but it gave me strong values and memories that I still carry today.


Teenage Years

     1My teenage years were a time of growth, change, and self-discovery. 2I started to understand more about my personality, strengths, and weaknesses. 3I was still shy, but I slowly became more comfortable speaking when necessary. 4From Grade 7 to Grade 12, I studied at La Consolacion in Deparo, Caloocan City. 5School became more challenging as the subjects became harder. 6I had to manage more assignments, projects, and deadlines. 7At first, it was overwhelming, but I learned how to organize my time better. 8I realized that discipline is important if I want to succeed. 9I joined some school activities, but I still preferred smaller groups rather than big crowds. 10I enjoyed spending time with close friends who understood my personality. 11My high school classmates were sometimes noisy and not always obedient, but they made school life interesting and unforgettable. 12Then the pandemic suddenly changed everything. 13Classes became online, and it felt strange not seeing classmates in person. 14I spent most of my time at home attending virtual classes. 15Because I stayed home most of the time, I gained weight since I was not very active. 16Even though online classes were difficult, I learned new skills like adjusting to technology and studying independently. 17I also spent time playing online games with friends, which helped me relax and stay connected to them. 18After the pandemic, returning to face-to-face classes felt both exciting and tiring. 19Senior high school had many unexpected moments and incidents that made it memorable. 20During these years, I learned how to commute alone, which helped me become more independent and responsible. 21My teenage years shaped my maturity and helped me prepare for the next stage of my life.


College Experience

     1I am now in college, and this stage of my life feels very different from before. 2College requires more effort, deeper thinking, and stronger responsibility. 3I have more freedom in managing my schedule, but that also means I must be more disciplined. 4Every day, I travel a long distance to school. 5The commute in the morning usually takes around two hours. 6Going home can take three to four hours, especially when there is traffic. 7The long commute is tiring, but it teaches me patience and endurance. 8There are days when I feel exhausted, but I remind myself why I am studying. 9I enjoy learning new topics, especially when they challenge me to think critically. 10I like solving problems and working on projects because they help improve my skills. 11When I have free time, I play games like Roblox and GTA to relax. 12I also create small projects in Roblox Studio, which allows me to be creative and explore ideas. 13College has helped me discover what I am good at and what I enjoy doing. 14I am slowly becoming more confident in my abilities. 15I have friends who help and support me, especially during difficult schoolwork. 16Although it is harder to meet old friends because of different schedules, I still value the time we spend together. 17I try my best to balance school, hobbies, and rest. 18Some days are stressful, but I am learning how to manage pressure better. 19Seeing my skills improve little by little makes all the hard work worth it. 20Even though college life is challenging, I believe it is helping me grow into a stronger and more capable person.
