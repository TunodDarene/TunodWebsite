FREE vs PAID apps

by: Darene Hanz H. Tunod

Introduction

When I download apps, whether it’s a game or a photo or video editing tool, I always check the reviews first. Reading reviews helps me understand if the app works properly or if it has many problems. If users are complaining about crashes, bugs, or poor customer support, I usually avoid it. Reviews also help me see if the features are actually useful or just advertised well. I usually prefer free apps because they do not cost anything. As a student, saving money is important. Free apps allow me to try different tools without worrying about spending. However, free apps sometimes come with limitations such as ads, watermarks, or locked features. Paid apps, on the other hand, often provide better performance and more advanced tools. They usually remove ads and offer a smoother and more professional experience. However, they can also be expensive, especially when they require monthly subscriptions. Because of this, choosing between free and paid apps is not always simple.

Before You BUY

Before deciding whether to buy an app or not, I like to carefully weigh the pros and cons. I usually ask myself: Is this worth the price? Will I use it often? Does it really improve my work? For free apps, I can easily download and test them as long as I have enough storage space. If I do not like them, I can simply uninstall them. However, paid apps require more serious thinking. Once money is involved, the decision becomes more important. On platforms like Steam, refunds are sometimes available if certain conditions are met, which gives buyers some protection. Still, I prefer waiting for reviews before buying anything. I avoid pre-orders because sometimes the final product does not meet expectations. For professional software like Photoshop or Premiere Pro, the quality is very high and widely trusted. However, the subscription model can be expensive in the long run. Paying monthly fees may not always be practical for students. Because of this, I look for discounts, student versions, or free alternatives before committing. While some people turn to piracy to access paid software, this option is illegal and risky. Pirated software can contain viruses or malware that may harm a computer. It can also lead to legal consequences. Because of these risks, it is better to explore legitimate free alternatives whenever possible.

Comparing Alternatives

There are many paid programs that are considered industry standards. They offer advanced features, regular updates, and strong customer support. However, there are also free and open-source alternatives that can perform similar tasks. While they may not always have the same level of polish, they are still very capable and useful for students.

  1. Paid Software
    • Adobe Photoshop
    • Adobe Premiere Pro
    • Adobe Illustrator
    • Microsoft Word / Excel / PowerPoint
    • Final Cut Pro
    • AutoCAD
  1. Free Software
    • GIMP
    • DaVinci Resolve
    • Inkscape
    • LibreOffice
    • Blender
    • FreeCAD
Final Verdict

There are many software applications available that can suit our needs and preferences. Choosing whether to buy a program or use a free alternative depends on budget, purpose, and long-term use. If the paid software will be used frequently and professionally, it may be a good investment. However, if it is only for basic tasks, a free alternative might be enough. Some paid software is not always worth the high cost, especially when similar tools are available for free. Free programs today are becoming more powerful and competitive. They may not always match the exact quality of paid software, but they can accomplish most tasks effectively. It is also important to consider safety and ethics when choosing software. Using legitimate applications protects your computer from security risks and avoids legal problems. In the end, the best choice is the one that balances cost, performance, and reliability. By making smart decisions, I can save money while still producing quality work.
